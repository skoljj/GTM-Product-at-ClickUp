This isn't addition. It's exponentiation. The quality of human input sets the base. The sophistication of AI application sets the exponent. Together they produce value that neither achieves alone — and the gap between linear and exponential is where real outcomes live.

02 — Components
HI
Human Intelligence
HI = f ( creativity, effort, focus )
The irreducible human contribution. Not raw IQ — intelligence applied through deliberate action. The base of the equation: without it, the exponent has nothing to amplify.
  • CreativityThe ability to frame problems others haven't seen. Connecting disparate ideas into novel solutions.
  • EffortSustained, directed energy. Not busy-work — the willingness to push through ambiguity and iterate toward clarity.
  • FocusThe discipline to prioritize ruthlessly. Knowing what matters, what to ignore, and when to go deep.
AI
Artificial Intelligence
AI = f ( context, proximity, leverage )
The multiplier. Not a tool that replaces thinking — one that compounds it. The exponent: the closer and more contextually precise, the more powerful the amplification.
  • ContextAI is only as powerful as the information it operates on. Rich, structured context unlocks exponential capability.
  • ProximityHow close AI sits to the moment of decision or action. Embedded in the workflow, not bolted on after.
  • LeverageThe ratio of output to input. Small, precise interventions that create outsized outcomes across the system.
V
actual Value
V = Σ ( outcomes, results, impact )
The sum consequence. Not a single metric — the aggregate of everything that matters, measured across the dimensions that define success.
  • OutcomesImmediate, observable changes. The deal closed. Ramp time decreased. The rep hit quota. Concrete, measurable shifts.
  • ResultsPatterns that emerge across outcomes. Trend lines, not snapshots. Consistent attainment, not one-off wins.
  • ImpactSecond-order effects. Culture shifts, capability building, compounding organizational intelligence over time.
03 — Two Reads of the Same Idea

The framework expresses itself two ways — one exponential, one multiplicative. Different syntax, same conviction: human capability and AI capability are not independent variables. They compound.

Read 01 — Exponential

The Power Expression

applied HI  ^  applied AI  =  actual V

Human intelligence is the base. Artificial intelligence is the exponent. "Applied" does the heaviest lifting in both terms — raw potential means nothing without execution. The word "actual" on value is deliberate: this framework doesn't count potential or intent. It counts what shipped, what changed, what compounded.

Read 02 — Multiplicative

The Quotient Expression

Human Quotient × AI Quotient = Real Outcome

A "quotient" implies a score — something measurable, something improvable. Your human quotient (how well people execute) multiplied by your AI quotient (how well systems amplify) equals the real outcome. Not the theoretical one. Not the one in the deck. The one in the pipeline, the board report, the culture.

04 — The Equation
HIAI = V
The relationship is exponential, not additive.
This changes everything about how we build enablement.

Why Exponentiation

If HI = 2 and AI = 10, addition gives you 12. Exponentiation gives you 1,024. The gap between organizations that bolt AI on and those that compound through it will be orders of magnitude — not incremental percentages. Enablement becomes a product discipline: you're engineering the base and calibrating the exponent.

Why "Applied" & "Actual"

Raw intelligence — human or artificial — is potential energy. Applied intelligence is kinetic. It's the difference between knowing MEDDPIC and running it instinctively in live discovery. Between having Gong data and surfacing the exact coaching insight when a manager needs it. And "actual" value means we measure what shipped, not what was planned. Application is where theory becomes outcome.

05 — Operating Principles
01

Raise the base first

Before adding AI, ensure the human intelligence layer is strong. Clear processes, well-framed problems, skilled practitioners. A higher base always beats a higher exponent on a weak foundation.

02

Proximity over power

An imperfect AI intervention at the point of need beats a sophisticated one delivered after the fact. Embed intelligence into workflows, not slide decks. Closer to action, greater the compound.

03

Measure the sum, not the parts

Value is outcomes plus results plus impact. A deal closed through a shortcut is an outcome, not a result. Sustained attainment is a result. Culture change is impact. Optimize for all three.

04

Context is the constraint

The bottleneck is almost never AI capability. It's the quality, structure, and timeliness of the context it receives. Invest in context architecture before model sophistication.

05

Quotients are trainable

Neither the human quotient nor the AI quotient is fixed. Both improve through deliberate investment — coaching, tooling, workflow design, feedback loops. Real outcomes are engineered, not hoped for.

06

Actual over theoretical

The words "actual" and "real" aren't decorative. They're a commitment to measuring what happened, not what was planned. Decks don't close deals. Dashboards don't change behavior. Shipped capability does.

Conversion Rates
The measurable handoffs between stages — where the GTM Product org drives impact.
CR1
Lead → MQL
Awareness → Education
Of everyone who becomes aware of ClickUp, what percentage engages enough to be considered marketing-qualified? This captures the transition from passive awareness to active interest — content consumption, webinar attendance, free workspace creation, or other intent signals that indicate a prospect is worth nurturing further.
Primary owner: Kyle Coleman (GVP Marketing) — content, PLG sign-ups, digital campaigns, intent signals
Action Layer Connection
Largely outside the GTM P&P function's direct scope. However, the action layer supports this through field intelligence — surfacing what messaging resonates in Gong calls back to Marketing to refine top-of-funnel positioning.
CR2
MQL → SQL
Education → Selection
Of those marketing-qualified leads, what percentage does the sales team accept as genuinely worth pursuing? This is the XDR qualification moment — the handoff from marketing to sales pipeline. It measures whether MQLs are actually the right fit (ICP match, timing, budget) or just noise.
Primary owner: XDR team + Kyle Coleman's org — qualification, outbound, hand-off to AEs
Action Layer Connection
The action layer provides XDR managers with qualification scorecards and coaching briefs — Gong analysis of discovery calls, ICP fit signals from Salesforce, and pipeline development playbooks sized for the XDR motion.
CR3
SQL → Won
Selection → Commit
Of qualified opportunities, what percentage closes? This is the win rate — the conversion the case study is most focused on. It's where MEDDPIC discipline, discovery quality, demo effectiveness, IER positioning, and certified agent selling all live. Currently below industry benchmarks at ClickUp, this is the highest-leverage CR for the GTM P&P function to improve.
Primary owner: Matt Bauman (Commercial) + Jeff de Reuter (Enterprise) — AEs and FLMs close deals
Action Layer Connection
This is the action layer's primary target. Coaching intelligence agents surface MEDDPIC gaps per deal, FLM inspection dashboards highlight at-risk opportunities, in-workflow nudges prompt reps when deals stall, and expansion/certified agent playbooks are triggered by pipeline signals — all through the 17 Commercial and 4→9 Enterprise FLMs.
CR4
Won → Active
Commit → Onboard
Of closed deals, what percentage actually gets to first value? This is the pivot point of the bowtie — the IER (Intake, Execution, Report) motion. A deal can be "won" but if the customer never fully onboards, builds their workspace, and achieves first workflow impact, they churn at renewal. This CR measures time-to-first-value.
Primary owner: Behezad Mehta (GVP Solutions & Success) — CS, TAMs, Implementation via IER
Action Layer Connection
The action layer supports the handoff by ensuring AEs properly set expectations during the sale (coached via Gong patterns), that onboarding playbooks are segment-sized, and that post-sales FLMs have visibility into activation milestones. The onboarding agent tracks customer progress and surfaces at-risk implementations.
CR5
Active → Renew
Onboard → Impact
Of active customers realizing value, what percentage renews? This is the retention motion — CSMs monitoring health scores, TAMs managing broad 1:100 portfolios, and the post-sales team ensuring customers aren't drifting into churn risk. ClickUp's self-serve base adds complexity since many customers renew (or don't) without ever talking to a human.
Primary owner: Post-Sales Ops (Kevin Lou) + CSMs/TAMs — health scores, retention, satisfaction
Action Layer Connection
The action layer here supports post-sales FLMs with customer health dashboards, renewal risk alerts, and playbooks for at-risk accounts. This is a later-phase priority for the GTM P&P function — commercial and enterprise sales are the H1 focus.
CR6
Renew → Expand
Impact → Grow
Of renewing customers, what percentage grows their spend? This is the expansion playbook — upsell to Certified Agents, cross-sell Super Agents, expand seats, upgrade tiers. ClickUp currently only assigns half its expandable install base to the sales team. The other half gets a digital AI experience or is managed by TAMs at 1:100 ratios.
Primary owner: AEs (expansion) + CSMs (advocacy) — cross-sell, upsell, seat expansion
Action Layer Connection
The action layer triggers expansion plays using whitespace analysis from Salesforce, usage propensity models, and certified agent opportunity detection. FLM dashboards show which reps have expansion pipeline, and coaching briefs surface account-level expansion signals from Gong calls. The expansion playbook is a Phase 2 capability launch on the roadmap.
